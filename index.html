PROJECT MANAGEMENT DEFINED

  • DOCS SPREADSHEETS / / Download Assessment 2

    • Project Management is defined as the discipline that involves 'initiating, planning, executing, and controlling the work of a team of people towards the achievement of a specific goal, or sets of goals.' These goals could be the development or production of unique products, services, or some other metric improvements, all of which are expected to deliver additional value. Through project management, activities are conducted using various project management tools and task management software, skill sets, knowledge, methodologies, and techniques in order to meet the requirements of the projects.


    The primary aim of project management is to ensure the project's objectives are met within specified constraints. The definition above already marks the difference between Project Management and Program management. Project Management has well-defined scopes and resources, whereas in Program Management, these aspects could be more adaptable. Project Management concerns a singular project, while Program Management involves overseeing multiple projects simultaneously.


    The Goals


    FIRST: What are the goals of the project? What does it ultimately want to achieve upon its closing? You have to take into account the needs of the stakeholders, or the people who have an interest in your project and its results. Will the project’s completion mean the satisfaction of their needs? Will it be able to provide a solution to their problems? Obviously, before you can set your project goals, you have to:


  • Identify your stakeholders: They may be your investors or sponsors of the project, the employees or members of the organization, the customers or end-users, or the general public as a whole.
  • Identify the needs of these stakeholders: Keep in mind that stakeholders have differences, and that includes their needs. The needs of the investors, for example, will be different from that of the customers. This may done through surveys and interviews.
  • Rank the needs from most important to least important: This is a way to set priorities, so you will know which ones to address first. The list will then enable you to set goals.

    • 6 Steps Approach to Effective Project Management


    1-INITIATION

    Proving the value and feasibility of the project.

    2-PLANNING

    Creating a plan that guides Execution and Monitoring and Control.

    3-EXECUTION

    Actioning the project and taking care of the people involved.



    4-MONITORING/CONTROLLING

    Ensuring that the project is running smoothly and handling the data.

    5-CLOSURE

    Giving feedback on the project's performance after it is been deployed and stakeholders have given their sign-off.

    6-POST-PROJECT OPTIMIZATION

    This all about implementing the changes that will improve the success of your future projects.
